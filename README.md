# FreshMart Retail Profitability & Discount Impact Analysis

## Project Overview

This project was carried out to investigate a profitability challenge within a retail business. While the company generated over **$12.6M in sales**, profitability was not growing at the same pace as revenue. The objective of this analysis was to identify where profit losses were occurring, understand the factors driving those losses, and provide actionable recommendations to improve business performance.

Using **Excel, SQL, and Power BI**, I performed a complete end-to-end analysis covering data cleaning, business understanding, root cause analysis, profitability analysis, discount evaluation, and dashboard development.

The key business questions were:

- Which categories contribute most to profitability?
- Which products are driving losses?

## Analysis Process

### 1. Executive Performance Analysis

I started by analyzing overall business performance using key metrics such as:

- Total Sales
- Total Profit
- Profit Margin
- Total Orders
- Average Discount
- Total Quantity Sold

Although overall sales performance was strong, category-level analysis revealed an important issue.

---

### 2. Category Analysis

Furniture generated strong revenue but significantly lower profits compared to other categories.

This raised an important business question:

> Why is Furniture generating high sales but producing weak profitability?

This became the starting point for deeper investigation.

---

### 3. Sub-Category Investigation

Drilling into Furniture revealed that **Tables** was the primary loss-making sub-category.

Although table products generated substantial sales revenue, they consistently recorded negative profits.

This indicated that the problem was not the entire Furniture category, but a specific product group within it.

---

### 4. Regional Analysis

After identifying Tables as the major source of losses, I investigated regional performance.

The analysis showed that regions such as:

- South
- Central
- Southeast Asia

contributed heavily to table-related losses, while some regions remained profitable.

This revealed that the profitability issue was concentrated in specific locations rather than being evenly distributed across the business.

---

### 5. Discount Impact Analysis

To understand why Tables were losing money, I analyzed the relationship between discounts and profitability.

Products were grouped into:

- Low Discount (≤ 20%)
- High Discount (> 20%)

The findings showed that high-discount transactions generated:

- More orders
- More units sold

However, they also generated:

- Lower revenue
- Negative profit margins
- Significant profit losses

This demonstrated that although discounts increased customer demand, they were reducing overall profitability.

---

### 6. Product Risk Analysis

To identify products requiring immediate attention, products were classified into:

- High Risk
- Medium Risk
- Low Risk

based on their discount levels and profitability performance.

This helped highlight specific table products that consistently generated losses and required pricing or discount strategy review.

---

## Key Findings

- Furniture generated strong sales but weak profitability.
- Tables were responsible for the majority of losses within Furniture.
- High discounts increased demand but reduced profitability.
- Low-discount transactions generated stronger profit margins.
- Certain regions contributed significantly to table-related losses.
- Excessive discounting was identified as the primary driver of profit leakage.

## Business Recommendations

- Reduce excessive discounts on loss-making table products.
- Review pricing strategies for underperforming products.
- Monitor profitability at both product and regional levels.
- Focus promotional efforts on products with stronger margins.
- Continuously track high-risk products to prevent future profit leakage.

---

## Conclusion

This analysis revealed that the business did not have a sales problem—it had a profitability problem.

While aggressive discounting increased customer demand and sales volume, it also reduced revenue and profit margins, particularly within the Tables sub-category. By optimizing discount strategies and focusing on high-risk products, the business can improve profitability without necessarily increasing sales volume.
